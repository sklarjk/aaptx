Define \(\phi: G/K\to G/N\) by \(\phi(aK)=aN\text{.}\) We have that \(\phi\) is well defined: indeed, let \(aK=bK \in G/K\text{.}\) Then by Statement 6 of TheoremÂ 7.2.4, we have \(aN=bN\text{,}\) that is, \(\phi(aK)=\phi(bK)\text{.}\) So \(\phi\) is well defined. \(\phi\) is clearly onto, and we have

\begin{align*} \Ker \phi\amp =\{aK\in G/K\,:\,\phi(aK)=N\}\\ \amp =\{aK\in G/K\,:\,aN=N\}\\ \amp =\{aK\in G/K\,:\,a\in N\}\\ \amp =N/K. \end{align*}

So the desired results hold, by the First Isomorphism Theorem.
